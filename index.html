UniPano: Panoramic Video Generation with Masked Diffusion Transformer

UniPano: A panoramic (ERP) video generation model supporting \textbf{unified control inputs}. By providing text, single-frame panoramic images, or NFoV video as input (left), our UniPano generates high-quality panoramic videos with consistent spatial and temporal coherence (right).

Abstract

With the surge in immersive experiences like AR and VR within the metaverse, panoramic video has become a focal point in research and applications. Capturing high-quality panoramic video is often prohibitively expensive, yet recent advancements in video generation models have shown strong potential across general video contexts. While some studies have explored panoramic video generation using text or sliding window control methods, a unified framework that integrates diverse control modalities is still lacking. To bridge this gap, we propose UniPano, a panoramic video generation framework built on the mask DiT method. our UniPanoâ€™s core innovation lies in its ability to unify multiple control modalities for panoramic video generation, including text prompts, single panoramic images, and narrow field-of-view (NFoV) videos, creating a versatile framework in this domain. To further improve both pixel-level and semantic-level spatiotemporal consistency, we design two loss functions tailored to panoramic motion patterns, along with an edge recovery technique based on phase-shift alignment.
